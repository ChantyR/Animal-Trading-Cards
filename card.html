Humboldt Penguin

As part of a promotion for the Japanese anime "Kemono Friends" (Animal Friends), anthropomorphized standees of various animals were placed in exhibits in Japanese zoos. Peculiarly, a particular Humboldt penguin, named Grape-kun, began exhibiting strange behavior towards the standee---which was recognized as mating dances Humboldt penguins perform for a partner, and when the standee was removed as the advertising campaign had finished, Grape-kun was visibly upset. The standee was put back in place, and Grape-kun went down in history as the penguin who loved an anime wife.

  • Scientific Name: Spheniscus humboldti
  • Habitat: Southwest Coastal South America
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years in captivity
  • Diet: Fish, primarily anchovies, herring, and smelt

Medium-sized for penguins, Humboldt penguins grow to just over 2 feet tall and weigh roughly 10 lbs. They are most easily identified by a black "breast" band and their beak, which is light-pink at the base. They are otherwise mostly blackish-gray on the upper part of their body, and mostly white on the lower part of their body, with a white stripe running on the head from around the eyes around the ears and then connects around the chin.
